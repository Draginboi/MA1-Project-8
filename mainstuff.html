Understanding P-Values

The p-value measures how compatible your data is with the null hypothesis. Lower p-values suggest your data would be rare if the null hypothesis were true.

Scenario: Testing a New Drug

Imagine a pharmaceutical company is testing a new drug to reduce blood pressure. Patients are randomly assigned to receive either the drug or a placebo, and blood pressure changes are recorded.

Key Points Explained

  • What has a probability of p?
    The p-value is the probability of observing results at least as extreme as yours, assuming the null hypothesis is true.
  • Why does a low p-value indicate the null hypothesis is likely false?
    Because your observed data would be very unlikely under the null model.
  • What is statistical significance?
    A result is “statistically significant” if its p-value falls below a pre-set threshold (commonly 0.05).
