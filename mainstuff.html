Understanding P-Values

P-values help us see how likely it is to get our results if the null hypothesis were true. They're about the data, not the truth of the hypothesis itself!

Scenario: Coin Flip Challenge

Suppose someone claims they have a magic coin that lands heads 70% of the time. You test it with 100 flips and get 68 heads.
Your calculated p-value = 0.04.

Breaking It Down

- A p-value of 0.04 means: if the coin was normal (50/50), there's a 4% chance of getting results like this.
- Small p-value? Unlikely if normal. Maybe the coin really is special?

The Big Picture

Low p-values suggest surprising results under the null hypothesis, but they don't *prove* anything! Always combine p-values with context and reasoning!
