Understanding P-Values

P-values show how surprising your data is if the null hypothesis were true. They’re about the data, not proving the hypothesis!

Scenario: Coin Flip Challenge

Someone claims their coin lands heads 70% of the time. You flip 100 times and get 68 heads. p-value = 0.04.

Breaking It Down

- p = 0.04 → 4% chance of ≥68 heads if the coin were fair.
- Small p? Fair-coin idea looks shaky!

The Big Picture

Low p-values suggest surprising results under H₀—but they don’t prove anything. Always combine with context and study quality!

What a P-Value is NOT

A p-value is not the probability that the null hypothesis is true.
It’s not the chance your results happened purely by luck. It’s the probability of data as extreme (or more) under H₀.

Final Thought

P-values ask "how weird is this data if the null is true?"
But the answer still depends on how well your study was done.
